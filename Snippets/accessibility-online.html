La Trobe University is committed to providing equitable access to education under the Disability Discrimination Act 1992, the Disability Standards for Education 2005 and the Carer Recognition Act 2010 to ensure that students who identify as Autistic, ADHD, Dyslexic and/or another minority neurotype, or live with a mental health condition, ongoing medical condition or disability (including physical, neurological, intellectual, sensory, acquired brain injury, or specific learning difficulty) can access and participate in education on the same basis as other students.

If you experience any difficulty accessing your subject, please notify your subject coordinator or tutor, and/or contact the AccessAbility Hub.

If you identify as a minority neurotype, or live with a mental health condition, ongoing medical condition or disability or you need support as a carer, AccessAbility Advisers can help you meet the requirements of your course through:

  • Advice on managing university study
  • Implementing reasonable adjustments, including alternate assessment/exam arrangements, assistance negotiating extensions, placement arrangements etc.
  • Information on assistive technology options
